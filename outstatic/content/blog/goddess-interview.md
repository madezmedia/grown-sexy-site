## The Voice of a Movement

She walks into the room with the kind of confidence that only comes from living life, making mistakes, learning lessons, and choosing to rise anyway. **GODDE$$** - stage name, movement, and mindset - is redefining what it means to be a woman in music after 30.

We sat down with her to discuss her journey, her artistry, and why she's become the perfect voice for The Grown & Sexy Movement.

---

### G&S: Let's start at the beginning. When did GODDE$$ the artist really emerge?

**GODDE$$:** Honestly? She was always there, but I was too afraid to let her out. I spent my 20s in corporate America, playing it safe, doing what I was "supposed" to do. Good job, 401k, benefits - all of that. But music was always calling me.

I walked away from that life at 32. Scariest thing I've ever done. But I realized I was living someone else's dream, not mine. GODDE$$ emerged when I finally gave myself permission to be who I really am.

### G&S: Your music speaks directly to women 30+. That's intentional, right?

**GODDE$$:** Absolutely. The music industry is obsessed with youth, and I get it - young sells. But what about the rest of us? What about women who've lived, loved, lost, and learned? Where's our anthem?

I'm 35 now. I know things I didn't know at 25. I love differently. I move differently. I demand more from life because I understand my worth. That wisdom, that confidence - it's sexy. It's powerful. And it deserves a soundtrack.

### G&S: Talk about your sound. How would you describe it?

**GODDE$$:** Neo-soul with an edge. R&B with substance. Hip-hop influence because that's my generation. But really, it's just honest music.

I don't do gimmicks. I don't follow trends. I make music that I want to hear - music that reflects the complexity of being a grown woman. Sometimes that's sensual. Sometimes it's empowering. Sometimes it's vulnerable. It's all real.

### G&S: "Grown Woman Anthem" became huge. Did you expect that response?

**GODDE$$:** [Laughs] Not at all! I wrote that song in my apartment one night feeling frustrated about always being told I was "too old" or "too much" or "too opinionated." I was like, you know what? I'm a grown woman and I'm not apologizing for it.

I recorded it, put it out, and it just... resonated. Women started sharing their stories - how they needed that reminder, how they felt seen. That's when I realized this was bigger than music. It's a movement.

### G&S: You've become the featured artist for The Grown & Sexy Movement. What does that mean to you?

**GODDE$$:** It's perfect alignment. What you're building here - spaces for mature adults to connect, celebrate, and be themselves - that's what my music is about. We're creating culture for people who've been overlooked.

When I perform at your events, I see women in their 30s, 40s, 50s singing every word, feeling seen and celebrated. That's what it's all about. This partnership isn't just business - it's purpose-driven.

### G&S: What's the creative process like for you?

**GODDE$$:** It varies. Sometimes a lyric hits me out of nowhere and I have to record a voice memo immediately. Other times I'm in the studio with producers vibing out, and we build something from a feeling.

But every song has to pass the honesty test. If I wouldn't say it in a conversation with my best friend, it doesn't make the album. My music is an extension of who I am. Authenticity is non-negotiable.

### G&S: Who are your musical influences?

**GODDE$$:** Erykah Badu taught me that weird is beautiful and staying true to yourself is revolutionary. Jill Scott showed me that poetry and vocals can create magic. Mary J. Blige demonstrated raw emotion and vulnerability as strength.

But honestly? My biggest influence is life. Every heartbreak, every triumph, every lesson learned - that's what informs the music.

### G&S: What's next for GODDE$$?

**GODDE$$:** I'm working on my debut full-length album right now. It's called *"Elevation"* and it's about the journey of rising - professionally, personally, spiritually.

I'm also planning an acoustic tour hitting intimate venues across the country. I want to connect with people face-to-face, hear their stories, create those moments of shared experience.

And of course, continuing to work with The Grown & Sexy Movement to create unforgettable experiences for the community.

### G&S: Any advice for women who feel like they're "too old" to pursue their dreams?

**GODDE$$:** [Leans forward intensely] You're not too old. You're exactly the right age. Everything you've experienced has prepared you for this moment.

I started my music career "late" by industry standards. But I wouldn't change a thing. The woman I am now - confident, focused, clear about who I am - she's the one who needed to make this music.

Your timeline is your timeline. Stop comparing. Start creating. The world needs what only you can offer, and it doesn't matter if you're 25, 35, 45, or 65. Just start.

### G&S: Last question - what does "grown and sexy" mean to you?

**GODDE$$:** It's knowing who you are and not apologizing for it. It's confidence without arrogance. Sensuality without seeking validation. Strength without hardness. Wisdom earned through experience.

It's understanding that you're a work in progress and that's beautiful. It's celebrating yourself while supporting others. It's living authentically, loving fully, and refusing to dim your light for anyone.

That's grown and sexy to me.
